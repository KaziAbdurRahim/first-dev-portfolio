Voices of Freedom
                In the heart of Dhaka, voices rise,
                A chorus of youth, their spirits high.
                For justice, for change, they march in stride,
                Their peaceful plea, a nation's pride.

                They stand for rights, with banners unfurled,
                A new chapter in freedom, bravely heralded.
                In unity, they claim their future's mold,
                A tale of courage, by students told.
                
            

    Echoes at the Gate
In shadows cast by broken dreams,
Where youthful voices silent scream.
Beneath the skies of Dhaka's grief,
A tale unfolds, beyond belief.

Innocence met with brutal might,
A massacre beneath the night.
For justice sought, their lives they gave,
Now rest they do, in early grave.

The streets once filled with vibrant cries,
Now echo with the mothers' sighs.
A nation's heart, so deeply scarred,
By violence cruel, and justice marred.

The blood-stained soil, a testament,
To dreams and hopes so violently spent.
Yet in the whispers of the breeze,
Their courage lives, and won’t appease.

We mourn their loss, but stand anew,
With burning hearts and vision true.
For in their names, we fight the wrong,
In memory, we grow so strong.

Let not their sacrifice be vain,
From tears and blood, we break our chain.
In shadows deep, where justice died,
Their legacy, our beacon, guide.
                
In the shadowed corridors of despair, Where innocence and hope collide, Young voices tremble, hearts laid bare, Their pleas echo, unanswered, denied.

“Uncle, please open the gate,” they cry, Tears mingling with fear and desperation, But stone hearts remain unmoved, awry, As silence thickens, sealing their damnation.

The uncles, once protectors, now blind, Their own kin’s faces lost in the crowd, They watch, detached, as fate unbinds, The fragile threads of life, so cruelly endowed.

Blood stains the ground, a chilling hue, Echoes of anguish reverberate, And in their hollow eyes, a truth anew: The uncles, too, are children of hate.

For what legacy shall they leave behind? A legacy of apathy, of hearts turned cold, As innocence falls, shattered, maligned, And the gates remain shut, stories untold.

May shame weigh heavy upon their souls, For inaction speaks louder than words, And the cries of those lost in the folds Of indifference haunt the night like wounded birds.
